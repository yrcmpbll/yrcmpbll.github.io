I am an AI scientist at the Fraunhofer Society working at the intersection of knowledge-intensive natural language processing and innovation research. My work focuses on modeling how knowledge is created, transferred, and transformed, using large language models and deep representation learning to characterize innovation agents and improve knowledge flows.

I currently serve as Deputy PI of the Data Science for Innovation group and hold a permanent research position at Fraunhofer in Leipzig. Across these roles, I lead and mentor teams building digital tools for technology foresight, knowledge transfer, and innovation analytics for research and industry.

My background combines applied mathematics, information theory, and statistical inference from the Max Planck Society with hands-on machine learning and data engineering in collaborative research projects. Recent work spans automatic term extraction, taxonomy induction, career path prediction, company similarity, and other methods that help organizations understand and accelerate innovation.
