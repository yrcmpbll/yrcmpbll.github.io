The paper studies how vector representations can support matching incoming research tasks to skills in an organizational skill taxonomy. It compares language-model, word-embedding, and keyword one-hot representations for recommending suitable internal skills to research requests. The language-model-based approach performs best and suggests a path toward automated task-skill recommendation in research organizations.

Keywords: task-skill matching, skill taxonomy, vector representations, knowledge management, organizational recommendation
