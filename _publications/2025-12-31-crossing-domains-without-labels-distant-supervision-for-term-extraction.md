The paper addresses automatic term extraction under domain transfer, where annotated data are expensive and supervised methods generalize poorly. It introduces a multi-domain benchmark, uses a black-box LLM to generate pseudo-labels, fine-tunes LLMs for term extraction, and adds lightweight heuristics for document-level consistency. The resulting approach improves over previous methods on most evaluated domains and releases data and models.

Keywords: automatic term extraction, distant supervision, domain transfer, LLM pseudo-labeling, scientific and general domains
