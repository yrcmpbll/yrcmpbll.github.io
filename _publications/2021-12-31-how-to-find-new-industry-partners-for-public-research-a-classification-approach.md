The paper frames industry partner discovery for public research organizations as a statistical classification problem. Using Fraunhofer cooperation data and firm-level features based on text, industry codes, and economic variables, it compares classifiers such as logistic regression, support vector machines, and random forests. The results suggest that relatively simple classification methods can support large-scale screening of potential industry partners.

Keywords: industry partner selection, public research organizations, statistical classification, technology transfer, firm-level text features
