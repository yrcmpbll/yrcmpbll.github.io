The paper studies static and dynamic structure in a large two-mode university-industry cooperation network. Because of the network&apos;s size and complexity, it uses statistical proxies such as rank-size degree distributions and rolling Robins-Alexander clustering coefficients. The analysis finds power-law-like signatures in firm degrees and suggests that network stability emerges after an early phase of high clustering and cross-correlation.

Keywords: university-industry networks, two-mode networks, network topology, rank-size distributions, structural change
