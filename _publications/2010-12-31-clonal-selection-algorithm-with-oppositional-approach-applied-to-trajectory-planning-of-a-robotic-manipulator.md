The paper applies clonal selection algorithms, including an oppositional variant, to trajectory planning for a three-link robotic arm. The optimization targets point-to-point movement while considering criteria such as movement time, distance, torque, and obstacle avoidance. Simulations indicate that the oppositional clonal selection approach improves convergence and response quality over the classical version.

Keywords: robotic manipulator, trajectory planning, clonal selection algorithm, oppositional learning, metaheuristic optimization
