The paper proposes a quantum-information-theoretic approach to measuring system complexity. It builds on the Shiner-Davison-Landsberg framework, uses linear entropy for density operators, and incorporates tangle as an entanglement measure. The measure is applied to maximally entangled mixed states to explore complexity beyond classical Shannon-based and quantum Kolmogorov-style approaches.

Keywords: quantum information, complexity measures, linear entropy, entanglement, mixed quantum states
